Executive Summary & Administrative MandateThe T-HALO (Taiwan Holistic Analytics and Logical Operations) system is a high-density, full-stack regulatory intelligence platform designed to support post-market surveillance (PMS) and clinical safety decision-making for the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration (TFDA, 衛生福利部食品藥物管理署).1.1 The Post-Market Regulatory ChallengeUnder the Taiwan Medical Device Act (中華民國醫療器材管理法), medical devices are classified by risk (Class I, Class II, and Class III). Historically, tracking post-market adverse events has been hampered by three core systemic failure modes:Unstructured Data Ingestion (The "Noise" Problem): Adverse event notifications, manufacturer field safety notices, biomedical engine logs, and clinical complaints arrive in non-standardized formats—ranging from handwritten nurse charts, arbitrary comma-separated values (CSVs), nested semi-structured JSONs, to raw XML sensor telemetry.Delayed Signal Detection: Sifting through thousands of reports to detect a faulty component (such as a specific batch of Chinese-made capacitors or a micro-cracked lithium battery) typically requires weeks of manual forensic audit by specialized TFDA evaluators.Delayed Regulatory Enforcement: Issuing Administrative Warning Letters (公文函令), recall circulars, and corrective/preventive action (CAPA) templates currently relies on manual drafting, leading to gaps in public health defense.1.2 The T-HALO Design ParadigmT-HALO resolves these vectors by constructing an active digital twin registry that correlates medical device license asset mappings with clinical adverse event telemetry in real-time. By leveraging the advanced text-processing, complex pattern extraction, and strict-JSON schema output capabilities of the Gemini 3.1 & 3.5 Large Language Model (LLM) family—using the modern @google/genai TypeScript SDK—T-HALO transforms raw clinical noise into highly structured, actionable regulatory dockets.2.
